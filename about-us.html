WCEME

WCEME Conference

The World Congress of Emergency Medicine Educators (WCEME) is a premier international forum dedicated to the advancement of Emergency Medicine. Bringing together leading clinicians, academic faculty, experts, and healthcare administrators from across the globe, this conference aims to engage in cutting-edge discussions, hands-on workshops, and breakthrough research shaping the future of emergency care. This conference is organised by the Department of Emergency Medicine, Aarupadai Veedu Medical College, Vinayaka Mission's Puducherry Campus in collaboration with the Association of Emergency Medicine Educators. The theme is "Learning by Doing: Advancing Hands-On Training in Emergency Medicine".

EM

The Department of Emergency Medicine

Aarupadai Medical College and Hospital is proud to showcase its Emergency Medical Services (EMS), which stands as a benchmark in patient-centered emergency care and disaster management. Our EMS team plays a critical role across all phases of emergency response—preparedness, intervention, and recovery. The fully-equipped emergency department handles a wide spectrum of cases, including trauma, poisoning, cardiac events, burns, strokes, and pediatric emergencies, supported by a dedicated ICU managed by experienced emergency physicians available 24/7. With over a decade of expertise, our faculty lead cutting-edge clinical care, education, and disaster preparedness training. We offer diagnostics—including CT, MRI, USG, and FAST scans—and operate ACLS ambulances outfitted with advanced life-saving equipment, providing free transport and first aid for underprivileged patients. As an institution committed to excellence, we conduct continuous training in BLS/ACLS (per AHA standards), mass casualty simulations, and protocol evaluations to align with international best practices. Aarupadai's EMS represents a model of clinical leadership, innovation, and compassionate care in emergency medicine.

AEME

The Association of Emergency Medicine Educators

The Association of Emergency Medicine Educators (AEME India) is a dynamic organization dedicated to advancing education, research, and collaboration within the field of emergency medicine. The association has rapidly become a prominent force in shaping the training and development of emergency medicine professionals. The association's mission is to foster excellence in emergency medicine education by promoting innovative teaching methodologies, encouraging research initiatives, and facilitating networking among educators in the field.

AVMC

Aarupadai Veedu Medical College

Aarupadai Veedu Medical College & Hospital is a constituent college of Vinayaka Mission's Research Foundation (Deemed to be University), established in the year 2000. It offers a MBBS program, Postgraduate courses, and PhD programs. In addition, the AVMC campus houses a nursing college, the School of Allied Health Sciences, the School of Behavioral and Rehabilitation, and the School of Physiotherapy. These institutions provide exceptional opportunities for interdisciplinary learning and research. AVMC provides comprehensive healthcare services, including specialized departments and advanced diagnostics. Actively engaging in community-oriented services, it operates urban and rural health centers. The campus boasts modern facilities, including a library, Wi-Fi-enabled areas, an auditorium, clinical skills labs, and a simulation center. Research is integral and supported by the Central Biomedical Research Laboratory, research committees, and collaborations with national and international institutions. Accredited by NAAC, NABH, and NABL, the institution emphasizes environmental sustainability, covering 33% of the campus with greenery, aligning with UNESCO's development goals.

VMRF

Vinayaka Mission's Research Foundation

VMRF is a pioneering and vibrant Research Foundation (Deemed to be University) recognized by the Ministry of Human Resources Development, Government of India, and endorsed by the University Grants Commission (UGC) under Section 3 of the UGC Act, 1956. VMRF encompasses an extensive academic landscape with 13 Colleges and 11 Schools spread across four campuses in Salem and Chengalpet Districts in Tamil Nadu, as well as Puducherry and Karaikal of the Union Territory of Puducherry and it is accredited by the National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC) with an A grade. With a legacy of over three decades, VMRF continues its journey, fostering enlightenment, empowerment, and innovation, shaping minds, and driving progress within the academic landscape and beyond.
